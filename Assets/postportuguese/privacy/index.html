PostPortuguese - Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 27, 2026

PostPortuguese is developed by Igor Ferreira. This policy describes how the app handles your data.

Data collected and stored on your device

PostPortuguese stores the following data locally on your device:

  • Social-media credentials. OAuth tokens for Mastodon and Bluesky are stored in the system Keychain. No passwords are stored by the app.
  • Post history. A record of posts you have sent (text, media filenames, alt text, timestamps, and account identifiers) is kept in a local database.
  • Scheduled posts. Drafts you schedule for later, including text, media, and target accounts, are stored in a local database until they are sent or deleted.
  • Media files. Images and videos you attach are held temporarily on disk until the post is sent.

If your device has iCloud enabled and the app has the CloudKit entitlement, post history and scheduled posts may sync to your private iCloud container. This data is visible only to your Apple Account and is governed by Apple's privacy policy.

No data is sent to any service other than those listed above. The app does not include any analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.

Data the app does not collect

  • The app does not collect personal information such as your name, email address, or location.
  • The app does not track your activity or build a profile.
  • The app does not share data with data brokers or advertisers.

Data retention and deletion

All app data is stored on your device (and, if enabled, in your private iCloud container). You can delete all data at any time by removing the app. To delete iCloud-synced data, use the iCloud storage management in your device settings.

Children's privacy

PostPortuguese does not knowingly collect data from children under 13.
